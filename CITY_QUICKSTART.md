# City Onboarding Quick Start

Get your city live on CivicQ in under 30 minutes!

## For City Officials

### Step 1: Register Your City (5 minutes)

1. Go to **https://civicq.org/city/register**

2. Fill out the registration form:
   - City name and state
   - Your contact information (as City Clerk or Administrator)
   - Official email domain (e.g., cityofboston.gov)
   - Links to verify your identity
   - Create a password

3. Click **Register City**

4. You'll receive a confirmation email

### Step 2: Wait for Verification (24 hours)

Our team will verify:
- Your email domain matches the official city domain
- Documentation confirms your role
- You're an authorized city official

You'll receive an email once verified.

### Step 3: Import Your Ballot (10 minutes)

Once verified, log in and go to the Setup Wizard:

**Option A: Manual Entry**
1. Click "Manual Entry"
2. Enter election date
3. Add each contest (Mayor, City Council, etc.)
4. Add candidates for each contest
5. Click "Import Ballot"

**Option B: API Import** (if your city uses VotingWorks or similar)
1. Click "API Import"
2. Select your election system
3. Enter API credentials
4. Click "Import"

### Step 4: Customize Your Branding (5 minutes)

1. Upload your city logo
2. Choose your city's colors
3. Preview the changes
4. Click "Save & Continue"

### Step 5: Invite Your Team (5 minutes)

Invite colleagues to help manage:
1. Enter their email addresses
2. Select their role (Admin, Editor, Moderator, or Viewer)
3. Click "Send Invitation"

They'll receive an email with a link to join.

### Step 6: Launch! (2 minutes)

1. Review everything
2. Click "Launch My City Portal"
3. Share your city's CivicQ URL with voters!

## URLs

- **City Registration:** `/city/register`
- **Setup Wizard:** `/city/{cityId}/setup`
- **City Dashboard:** `/city/{cityId}/dashboard`
- **Public City Page:** `/{city-slug}` (e.g., `/springfield-il`)

## Roles Explained

- **Owner** - You (City Clerk) - Full control
- **Admin** - Deputy or Assistant - Full administrative access
- **Editor** - Elections staff - Can import and edit ballots
- **Moderator** - Communications staff - Can moderate voter questions
- **Viewer** - Read-only - Can view dashboard

## What Happens After Launch?

1. **Voters Register** - Residents create accounts and verify their identity
2. **Voters Ask Questions** - Community submits questions for candidates
3. **Questions Get Ranked** - Voters upvote the most important questions
4. **Candidates Answer** - Candidates record video answers to top questions
5. **Informed Voting** - Voters make informed decisions

## Tips for Success

### Before You Start
- Gather your ballot data (contests, candidates)
- Have your city logo ready (PNG or SVG)
- Know your city's brand colors (hex codes)
- Identify who will help manage the portal

### Best Practices
- Import ballots at least 90 days before election
- Invite candidates to create accounts
- Share with voters via social media, city newsletter
- Monitor question activity weekly
- Respond to voter reports within 24 hours

## Common Questions

### Q: How much does it cost?
**A:** CivicQ is free for cities under 50,000 population. Larger cities pay based on usage.

### Q: How do we verify voters?
**A:** We offer multiple verification methods: SMS, email, voter roll lookup, or ID verification.

### Q: Can candidates answer all questions?
**A:** Candidates answer the top 100 ranked questions. They can also answer open questions.

### Q: What if someone posts inappropriate content?
**A:** Our AI moderator flags toxic content. Your team can review and remove if needed.

### Q: How do we promote this to voters?
**A:** We provide social media graphics, email templates, and website widgets.

### Q: What if we have a runoff election?
**A:** You can import multiple ballots for different election dates.

### Q: Can we export data?
**A:** Yes! Export questions, answers, and engagement data as CSV.
