**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** February 14, 2026
**Owner:** Product Team

## Vision Statement

**CivicQ turns campaigning into a standardized, verifiable public record of candidates answering the public's top questions, city by city, with integrity by design.**

---

## The Problem We're Solving

### Current State: Broken Local Elections

**Voters are confused and disengaged:**
- Local races have little media coverage
- Voters don't know what candidates stand for
- Campaign materials are flyers, yard signs, and attack ads
- No single source of truth for candidate positions
- Voter turnout for local elections is dismal (15-25%)

**Candidates face impossible choices:**
- Spend money on attention-grabbing marketing
- Or be invisible to voters
- Traditional voter guides have low response rates
- Debates are one-time events with limited reach
- Fear of being "clipped" out of context prevents authentic communication

**Cities struggle with civic engagement:**
- Voter confusion leads to complaints and low confidence
- No official infrastructure for candidate information
- Election offices field endless "who is running?" questions
- Accessibility is often an afterthought

---

## The CivicQ Solution

### What CivicQ Is

CivicQ is a **city-level candidate Q&A + ballot information platform** that makes campaigning look like **answering the public's top questions, on record, in one place**, instead of spending money to grab attention.

**Core Components:**

1. **Your Ballot as Home Screen:** Personalized ballot showing all local races and measures
2. **Community-Driven Questions:** Verified residents submit and rank questions that matter
3. **Structured Video Answers:** Candidates answer in standardized, time-boxed videos (no editing)
4. **Side-by-Side Comparison:** Watch all candidates answer the same question
5. **Transparent Moderation:** Clear rules, public audit logs, appeal process
6. **City-Branded Platform:** Official infrastructure, not third-party aggregation

---

## What Makes CivicQ Different

### Anti-Polarization by Design

Unlike social media platforms that reward outrage, CivicQ is built to **prevent** polarization:

- **No Comments:** No performative dunking or flame wars
- **No Engagement Algorithms:** Not a feed, it's a library
- **Portfolio Ranking:** Questions represent the whole community, not just loud factions
- **Structured Rebuttals:** Responses must cite specific claims, not enable attacks
- **Viewpoint Clustering:** Prevents one faction from dominating the top questions

---

### Integrity and Authenticity

CivicQ is **civic infrastructure**, not social media:

- **No Pay-to-Win:** No ads, no boosting, no sponsored content
- **No Editing:** All videos recorded in-app, time-boxed, standardized
- **Everything On Record:** Questions versioned, answers permanent
- **Provenance Tracking:** Cryptographic video signing, tamper detection
- **Transparent Moderation:** Published policies, public audit logs

---

### Accessibility First

CivicQ meets the needs of all voters:

- **Automated Captions:** All videos captioned for deaf/hard-of-hearing
- **Text Transcripts:** Searchable, readable alternative to video
- **Screen Reader Compatible:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliant
- **Plain Language:** 8th grade reading level target
- **Mobile-Responsive:** Works on any device

---

## Product Philosophy

### Core Principles

**1. Voters First, Always**
- Voters deserve clear, direct answers to their questions
- Platform design prioritizes voter information needs over candidate marketing
- No feature exists to benefit campaigns at voters' expense

**2. Fairness Over Favoritism**
- Equal visibility for all candidates
- No algorithmic boosting or suppression
- Transparent, predictable rules
- Nonpartisan by design

**3. Clarity Over Conformity**
- Structured answers that communicate positions clearly
- Not about sound bites or political theater
- Candidates can disagree respectfully
- Authenticity over polish

**4. Trust as a Feature**
- Privacy by design (minimal data collection)
- Transparent operations (public moderation logs)
- Auditable algorithms (explainable ranking)
- Open governance (independent advisory board)

**5. Civic Record, Not Social Feed**
- Permanent, archivable election record
- Not ephemeral or engagement-driven
- Library, not casino
- Education, not entertainment

## Product Risks and Mitigation

### Risk: Low Candidate Participation

**Impact:** Platform fails if candidates don't participate (empty Q&A is useless)

**Mitigation:**
- Fair, structured format reduces "gotcha" risk
- Social pressure (participation tracking)
- City endorsement signals legitimacy
- Rebuttal mechanism protects against false claims
- Case studies proving fairness

---

### Risk: Voter Engagement Below Threshold

**Impact:** Platform is irrelevant if voters don't use it

**Mitigation:**
- City-branded and promoted (official source)
- Civic org partnerships (distribution)
- Media embeds (reach beyond direct users)
- Social sharing (friends invite friends)
- Simple, fast UX (5-minute value delivery)

---

### Risk: Platform Becomes Polarization Tool

**Impact:** Design fails, platform amplifies division instead of reducing it

**Mitigation:**
- No comments or engagement algorithms
- Portfolio ranking (diverse viewpoints)
- Structured rebuttals (not free-form attacks)
- Moderation focused on civility and relevance
- Continuous monitoring and iteration

---

### Risk: Technical Failure During Election

**Impact:** Outage during critical period damages trust permanently

**Mitigation:**
- 99.9% uptime SLA
- Redundant infrastructure
- Static HTML fallback
- Pre-election load testing
- 24/7 monitoring and support
